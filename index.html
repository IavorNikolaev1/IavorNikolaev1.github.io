Mesh blocks and the Australian Census

Mesh blocks are bounded two dimensional areas that are overlay Australia’s geography in relation to population people, topology, geography and social features. Their area currently 57523 Statistical Areas Level 1 (SA1) and they are the smallest geographical unit that is currently used to segregate Australia’s land (200-800 population).
The Australian census, introduced by the Australian Government in 1911 is a survey that Australia’s adult population takes about many topics including, finance, location and education. The Australian Government then uses this data to strategically manage the nation federally, territorially and locally. After 1961, the Australian Government introduced the five-year interval, where the census was taken and reveiwed every five years. The latest census, conducted in 2016, was the first to introduce the SA1s in attempt to define Australia’s population more accurately and specifically.

Conclusion

Through the analysis and visualisation of both G08 and G29, we can rank all five groups in order of highest to lowest socioeconomic status:
1. Anglo Saxon
2. Western European
3. Middle Asian
4. Eastern European
5. Middle eastern
All groups except Middle Asians tend to densify in certain areas of Sydney, either north or south of Latte Line and this is highlighted in their Average Household income. The most likely reasons for the vast differentiation of household income in different ethnic groups is because it correlates to how wealthy the country of origin is itself. For example, countries in Great Brittan (GDP per Capita) are much wealthier and equally distributed to those in the middle east, thus the same trend is true in the areas of Sydney. Furthermore, the clustering of ethnic groups in Sydney may cause a cycle of relative poverty, as in Children born into a low socioeconomic culture will be promoted to continue to live in the same way.
